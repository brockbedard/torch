# TORCH Research: Star Player Mechanic

**Date:** 2026-03-22
**Status:** Research complete, ready for design decision

## 1. Cross-Game Research

### Marvel Snap — What Makes Powerful Cards Feel Different

Marvel Snap's design philosophy centers on **high-synergy driven decks** rather than individual card dominance. Key lessons:

- **Visual distinction is layered**: Cards earn upgraded borders, particle effects, and 3D animations through the variant/upgrade system. The art, 3D effects, and animation come together to make cards feel like collector's items.
- **Power through archetype, not raw stats**: Cards like Human Torch are powerful because they enable a *playstyle* (Move archetype), not because they have the highest numbers. The developers explicitly prefer synergy-driven strategies over generic power.
- **Build-around cards create identity**: Cards like High Evolutionary and Morgan offer unique playstyles that define a deck. The card *is* the deck's identity — this is the closest analog to a "star player."
- **Cosmetic variants are pure prestige**: Over 667 variant art styles exist purely for aesthetics. Premium feel comes from stylization + detail — "glossiness that signals quality."

**Takeaway for TORCH:** Star players should define a team's *identity and playstyle*, not just have bigger numbers. Visual treatment (gold border, special art, particle effects) is important for the premium feel but should be layered on top of a gameplay hook.

Sources: [Marvel Snap Complete Guide](https://gamerant.com/marvel-snap-guide-beginners-best-decks-cards-tips-tricks-strategies-pool/), [Inside the Art of Marvel Snap](https://www.marvel.com/articles/games/inside-the-art-of-marvel-snap), [Marvel Snap Variants Guide](https://marvelsnapzone.com/marvel-snap-variants-guide/)

### Madden X-Factor / Superstar Abilities — Star Players in Football Games

Madden's X-Factor system is the most directly relevant reference for TORCH:

- **Two-tier ability system**: Superstar abilities are *always active* (passive boosts to route running, pass rushing, etc.). X-Factor abilities are *dormant until activated* by meeting in-game conditions (e.g., complete 3 passes, get 2 sacks).
- **Activation creates narrative moments**: When an X-Factor activates, it feels like a player "getting hot." The opposing team must then *deactivate* it by forcing a turnover, sack, etc. This creates a tug-of-war.
- **Examples**: "Shutdown" (CB enters zone, tighter coverage + more INTs), "First One Free" (increased juke/spin success after activation).
- **Hero cards in MUT provide +3 OVR boost** to any player on your roster — a simple, visible power bump.
- **Chemistry bonuses** reward thematic team-building (e.g., former college teammates unlock bonus chemistry).

**Takeaway for TORCH:** The X-Factor activation/deactivation loop is brilliant for creating dramatic moments. A star player who "heats up" based on in-game performance — then can be "cooled down" by the opponent — creates genuine tension. The +3 OVR "hero boost" is the simplest possible implementation.

Sources: [Madden X-Factor Wiki](https://madden.fandom.com/wiki/Superstar_X-Factor), [Madden 25 X-Factors Guide](https://screenrant.com/madden-25-how-to-use-all-superstar-x-factors/), [Madden 26 X-Factors List](https://www.gamesradar.com/games/madden-nfl/madden-26-x-factors-superstars-list/)

### Fire Emblem / XCOM — Hero Units in Tactical Games

Both games feature hero units that feel fundamentally different from regular troops:

- **Fire Emblem**: Legendary Heroes grant elemental buffs to nearby allies. Duo Heroes pair two characters with a unique shared mechanic. The key design: heroes *amplify the team*, not just themselves.
- **XCOM hero classes** (Reaper, Templar, Skirmisher) each have a *unique action economy* — they can do things regular soldiers simply cannot (stealth scouting, melee chains, grapple repositioning). They feel powerful because they *break the rules* that other units follow.
- **Risk-reward dynamic**: XCOM's hero units attract overconfidence. Their power *tempts* you into risky plays, and the game's structure punishes overextension. This creates meaningful decisions.
- **Permanent death stakes**: In both games, losing a hero unit is devastating, which makes every decision with them feel weighty.

**Takeaway for TORCH:** The best hero unit design gives them a *rule-breaking ability* — something no other card can do. In TORCH terms, this could mean the star player ignores a normal constraint (e.g., can be played twice in a row, or their badge combo always fires regardless of play type).

Sources: [XCOM Hero Units](https://xcom.fandom.com/wiki/XCOM_Hero), [Fire Emblem Heroes Mechanics](https://feheroes.fandom.com/wiki/Mechanics), [Learning from XCOM Classes](https://lurkerablog.wordpress.com/2023/02/13/learning-from-xcom-3-classes-advancement-and-special-moves/)

### Clash Royale — Legendary vs. Common Cards

Clash Royale's rarity system reveals an important insight:

- **Rarity does NOT equal power**: Common cards like Skeletons and Knight dominate top-tier play. Legendaries are harder to *obtain and upgrade*, not inherently stronger.
- **Legendaries feel special through unique mechanics**: Each legendary has a distinctive ability (Electro Wizard's stun-on-deploy, Night Witch's bat spawning). They don't do "more damage" — they do *different things*.
- **The collect-and-upgrade loop** makes legendaries feel prestigious. It's about *investment and identity*, not raw power.

**Takeaway for TORCH:** Since TORCH has no collection/upgrade loop (teams are predetermined), the star player's specialness must come entirely from *in-game feel* — visual treatment + a unique mechanic. Simply having higher OVR is not enough; Clash Royale proves that uniqueness beats raw power for player satisfaction.

Sources: [Clash Royale Card Rarities Guide](https://clashdecks.com/guides/beginner/understanding-card-rarities), [Clash Royale Best Legendary Cards](https://skycoach.gg/blog/clash-royale/articles/best-legendary-cards)

### TFT / Dota Underlords — Star Units in Auto-Battlers

The auto-battler star system is about *investment payoff*:

- **TFT's star-up system** (3 copies = 2-star, 9 copies = 3-star) creates the core satisfaction loop: "I built this carry, itemized it perfectly, and now it tears through enemies."
- **Power spikes are dramatic**: In Dota Underlords, 3-star units received *more than double* stats vs. 2-star — a deliberate choice to make the power spike feel massive and rewarding.
- **The carry fantasy**: Auto-battlers let you identify ONE unit as your carry and build the entire team around supporting it. Items, synergies, and positioning all serve the carry.
- **4-star units** (TFT Set 12) are so rare they become the team's identity — opponents have to specifically plan around them.

**Takeaway for TORCH:** The "build around the carry" fantasy is extremely satisfying. TORCH's star player should feel like the *center of gravity* — the player you want to get the ball to (offense) or the player who anchors your defense. The team should feel like it's built to enable the star.

Sources: [TFT Game Design Analysis](https://medium.com/@ZiberBugs/game-design-analysis-teamfight-tactics-bc6eb5aafeff), [TFT 4-Star Guide](https://www.thegamer.com/teamfight-tactics-set-12-4-star-unit-guide/), [Dota Underlords 3-Star Buffs](https://dotesports.com/dota-2/news/making-three-star-units-in-dota-underlords-is-now-worth-it)

### Hades / Slay the Spire — Rare Cards and Power Moments

Both roguelikes use rarity to create defining moments:

- **Hades boon rarity** (Common → Rare → Epic → Heroic → Legendary/Duo) uses color coding (white → blue → purple → red → gold) with escalating visual and audio fanfare. Legendary and Duo boons get a *special visual effect on appearance*, signaling "this is important" before you even read the text.
- **Slay the Spire rare cards** have gold borders and are designed to be *build-defining*, not universally strong. Feed, Wraith Form, and Corruption each create completely different decks. The design philosophy: "only some problems are solved," preventing any choice from being obviously best.
- **Both games use audio-visual feedback** to make rare acquisitions feel momentous — screen flash, special sound, dramatic pause.

**Takeaway for TORCH:** Star player moments should have *escalating audio-visual feedback*. When the star player makes a big play, the celebration should be distinctly more dramatic than a regular player's. The gold border / special visual treatment is table stakes — the *moment of impact* is what creates the memory.

Sources: [Hades Boons Wiki](https://hades.fandom.com/wiki/Boons), [Slay the Spire Card Design Tips](https://www.cloudfallstudios.com/blog/2020/11/2/game-design-tips-reverse-engineering-slay-the-spires-decisions), [Slay the Spire Rare Cards](https://slay-the-spire.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Cards)

## 2. Design Patterns Summary

| Pattern | Games Using It | Applicability to TORCH |
|---------|---------------|----------------------|
| Visual-only distinction (border, art, effects) | All games | Baseline — necessary but not sufficient |
| Unique mechanic (does something others can't) | Clash Royale, XCOM, Marvel Snap | High — creates identity |
| Activation condition (earn your power) | Madden X-Factor, Hades | High — creates narrative arc |
| Passive always-on boost | Madden Superstar, Fire Emblem Legendary | Medium — simple but less exciting |
| Build-around / carry fantasy | TFT, Marvel Snap, Slay the Spire | Medium — limited by no-draft constraint |
| Escalating audio-visual feedback | Hades, Slay the Spire, Marvel Snap | High — cheap to implement, high impact |
| Clutch / comeback trigger | Madden X-Factor, Duel Masters | High — fits football drama perfectly |

## 3. Recommendation for TORCH

### Q: Visual-only or gameplay mechanic?

**Both, but the gameplay mechanic is essential.**

Visual-only treatment (gold border, special card art, star icon) is necessary but not sufficient. Every game studied uses visual distinction as the *baseline*, then layers a gameplay hook on top. Without a mechanic, the star player is just "the guy with the gold border" — forgettable after 2 games.

### Q: What's the simplest gameplay mechanic that creates meaningful decisions?

**Recommended: "Heat Check" — an activation/deactivation system inspired by Madden X-Factor.**

#### How It Works

Each star player has a **signature ability** that starts **dormant**. It activates when the star meets a condition during the game:

**Offensive Star Activation:**
- Star player is used on a play that gains 10+ yards, OR
- Star player's badge combo fires successfully

**Defensive Star Activation:**
- Star player is on the field for a turnover (INT/fumble), OR
- Star player is on the field for a sack or run stuff

**When Activated ("On Fire"):**
- Star player's OVR gets **+4 boost** (significant but not game-breaking)
- Star player's badge combo grants **+5 bonus yards** instead of the normal +2-4
- Visual: card gets a **pulsing flame border** + **ember particle effect**
- Audio: special sound cue on activation, enhanced snap sound when star is in play

**Deactivation ("Cooled Off"):**
- Offense: opponent forces a turnover or sack while star is on field
- Defense: opponent scores a TD while star is on field
- Star returns to dormant state (can be re-activated)

#### Why This Works

1. **Creates a narrative arc**: The star "heats up" during the game, creating a story. "Vasquez caught fire in the 2nd quarter and CT never looked back."
2. **Meaningful decisions**: Do you keep feeding the star to maintain the streak, or does that make you predictable? The opponent might counter your star's preferred play type.
3. **No draft needed**: The star is always on your team. The mechanic is about *in-game performance*, not team construction.
4. **Fits TORCH's existing systems**: Activation uses the badge combo and yardage systems already built. The OVR boost plugs directly into the snap resolver. Zero new engine systems required.
5. **Creates counterplay for the AI**: The AI can specifically target plays that counter the activated star, adding strategic depth to difficulty scaling.

#### Minimal Viable Version (if Heat Check is too complex for v1)

**Absolute simplest: "Star Boost" — always-on passive.**
- Star player gets a permanent **+3 OVR** and a **gold star icon** on their card
- Star player's badge combo grants **+2 bonus yards** on top of normal combo bonus
- No activation/deactivation logic needed
- Still feels different, just less dramatic

This can ship immediately and be upgraded to Heat Check later.

### Q: How should the star player be presented on the team select screen?

**Recommended approach:**

1. **Team select screen**: Each team card already shows a roster preview. The star players should be called out with:
   - A **gold star badge** next to their name
   - Their name in **--a-gold (#FFB800)** instead of white
   - A one-line **tagline** below their name (e.g., "Vasquez — *The Closer*" or "Crews — *The Shutdown*")

2. **Player draft screen**: Star players should be **pre-selected and locked** (gold border, star icon, "STAR" label). The player drafts their remaining 3 starters around the star. This reinforces the "build around the carry" fantasy from auto-battlers without adding draft complexity.

3. **Pre-game intro**: After coin toss, a brief **star player spotlight** (0.5s) showing both stars with their signature ability name. Sets the stage.

### Q: Should the star have a "signature moment" mechanic?

**Yes — but keep it simple. Recommend a "Clutch Factor" that layers on top of Heat Check.**

#### Clutch Factor

When the star player is **activated ("On Fire")** AND it's the **4th quarter or 2-minute drill**, the star gets an additional boost:

- **+2 more OVR** (total +6 over base when clutch + heated)
- **Badge combo auto-fires** regardless of play type (the star transcends the system)
- **Special commentary line**: "Vasquez is TAKING OVER this game!"
- **Enhanced celebration**: If the star scores a TD while clutch, the TD celebration gets a unique screen effect (e.g., golden flash instead of green, or slow-motion feel)

#### Why Clutch Factor Works

- Football's greatest moments are 4th-quarter comebacks and clutch drives
- It creates the story arc: dormant → activated → CLUTCH — escalating drama
- It rewards players who keep their star "hot" into the late game
- The badge-combo-always-fires rule is the "rule-breaking" mechanic that XCOM teaches us makes heroes feel special
- It's rare enough (must be heated + late game) to feel momentous, not routine

#### If Clutch Factor is too much for v1

Skip it entirely. Heat Check alone provides enough star player drama. Clutch Factor can be added in a future version as a "star player evolution."

## 4. Implementation Priority

### Phase 1 — Visual Foundation (can ship immediately)
- Gold star icon on star player cards
- Gold border treatment (thicker, --a-gold colored)
- Star player name in gold on team select
- "STAR" label on draft screen (pre-locked)
- Tagline on team select

### Phase 2 — Heat Check Mechanic
- Add `isStar` flag to player data
- Add `starActivated` state to gameState
- Activation/deactivation logic in snapResolver (hooks into existing badge combo and yardage checks)
- OVR boost when activated (+4)
- Pulsing flame border CSS animation on activated star card
- Activation sound effect (jsfxr)
- Enhanced commentary lines for star plays

### Phase 3 — Clutch Factor (future)
- 4th quarter / 2-minute drill detection (already exists)
- Additional OVR boost stacking
- Badge-combo-always-fires rule
- Unique star TD celebration
- Star player spotlight in pre-game

### Estimated Effort
- Phase 1: ~2 hours (CSS + minor data changes)
- Phase 2: ~4-6 hours (engine logic + UI + sound)
- Phase 3: ~2-3 hours (conditional logic + celebrations)

## 6. Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Star player feels OP when activated | +4 OVR is meaningful but not dominant (existing OVR range is 72-82, so +4 makes an 82 into 86 — strong but beatable) |
| Player always picks star player for every snap | Already incentivized by highest OVR — Heat Check just makes it *more* interesting by adding the activation quest |
| AI difficulty scaling broken by star | AI can target counter-plays specifically when opponent star is hot (Hard mode already does optimal countering) |
| Too many mechanics for a casual mobile game | Phase 1 (visual only) is zero complexity. Phase 2 adds one state toggle. Phase 3 is optional. |
| Stars are always the same players (no variety) | True for v1 with 2 teams. When more teams are added, each team's star creates variety. Could also rotate stars in daily puzzle mode. |
