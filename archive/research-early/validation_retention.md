# Validation: Situational Keys & Attribute Decay for Long-Term Retention

This document validates the "Situational Keys" and "Attribute Decay" mechanics for *Torch Football*, focusing on their impact on long-term player retention and tactical depth.

## 1. Situational Keys: The "Read-and-React" RPG Layer

"Situational Keys" transform football from a simple play-calling sim into a high-stakes "Pre-Snap" RPG phase.

### The Mechanic
*   **Visual Cues:** Before the snap, the defense (and offense) is shown "Keys"—visual or data-driven hints about the opponent's intent (e.g., "LT is in a pass-pro stance," "QB is looking at the play-clock").
*   **The Interpretation:** Players use their **Awareness (AWR)** and **Film Study** stats to "reveal" the true meaning of these keys.
*   **The Reward:** Correctly identifying a key grants a **Situational Bonus** (e.g., +15% Block Shedding for a Run Key). Failing to react or "guessing" wrong results in being "Out of Position."

### Impact on Retention
*   **Skill Ceiling:** It adds a "mastery" layer where veteran players learn the "language" of the game's animations and data patterns.
*   **Progression Hook:** Upgrading a player's **Intelligence** or **Awareness** provides immediate, tangible value by revealing more keys, making the RPG progression feel essential to on-field success.

## 2. Granular Attribute Decay: Empowered vs. Punished

Does losing "Technique" (Strength, Pass Block, Accuracy) make players feel bad? Research suggests that **how** it decays is more important than **what** decays.

### Empowered (The "Graceful Transition")
*   **Tension over Frustration:** Granular decay (losing 1-2 points per play) creates a "mounting tension." The player doesn't feel "robbed" of their star; they feel the "clock ticking" on their peak performance.
*   **Tactical Shift:** When a QB’s **Accuracy** decays but their **Arm Strength** remains, the player is forced to stop throwing deep and start using "check-downs." This "forced variety" makes the game feel more dynamic and less repetitive.
*   **Depth through Rotation:** By decaying *technique* (e.g., a Lineman's "Pass Block" rating), the game makes "Rotational Depth" a requirement. A fresh 75 OVR backup becomes more valuable than a 90 OVR starter who is at 50% condition.

### Punished (The "Hard Crash")
*   **Avoid the "Fraud" Feeling:** If decay is too fast or affects fundamental movement (e.g., a massive Speed drop), the player feels the game is "cheating" to make them lose.
*   **Mitigation Tools:** To keep decay feeling "empowering," the player MUST have tools to fight it:
    *   **Medical Staff Upgrades** (Slows decay).
    *   **"Second Wind" Abilities** (Temporary restoration).
    *   **Clear UI Feedback** (Players should see *why* they are failing).

## 3. Handling "Condition" for Tactical Depth

Successful RPGs and sports managers (*Football Manager*, *Retro Bowl*, *Darkest Dungeon*) use Condition as a **Resource Conversion** mechanic.

| Game | Approach to Condition | Tactical Depth Created |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Football Manager** | **Intensity vs. Longevity** | High-intensity tactics (Gegenpress) win games but destroy players. Managers must choose when to "rest" stars. |
| **Retro Bowl** | **Attribute Scaling** | Stamina determines the 4th-quarter "clutch" factor. You can't rely on the same "money play" all game. |
| **Darkest Dungeon** | **Stress as a Resource** | Mental "Condition" can break your tactical formation, forcing you to retreat or adapt to "afflicted" characters. |

### Key Takeaways for *Torch Football*:
1.  **Stamina as a Curve, not a Cliff:** Use a parabolic decay curve where the first 25% of fatigue has minimal impact, but the last 25% causes significant technique loss (fumbles, dropped passes, missed blocks).
2.  **Condition-Based Archetypes:**
    *   **The "Workhorse":** High Stamina, average stats. They stay at 90% performance all game.
    *   **The "Sprinting Star":** Elite stats that decay rapidly. They are "Game Changers" for 2 quarters but must be benched for the 3rd.
3.  **UI Feedback (The "Glow"):** Use visual indicators (sweat, heavy breathing, or a "flickering" stat bar) so the player *knows* a mistake was caused by fatigue rather than bad luck (RNG).

## 4. Final Verdict: Validation Status

*   **Situational Keys:** **VALIDATED.** It provides the "hook" for RPG progression and creates a "poker game" between players.
*   **Attribute Decay:** **VALIDATED (with Caution).** It must remain "granular" and "mitigatable." If decay makes players "unusable," it becomes a punishment. If it forces "tactical shifts," it becomes depth.
