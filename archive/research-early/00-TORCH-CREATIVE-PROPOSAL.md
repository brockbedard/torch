# TORCH: The Creative Proposal
## "What if every touchdown drive felt like Balatro's fire animation?"

*Synthesized from 5 parallel research tracks. Not a safe composite — an opinionated vision for what TORCH should become.*

---

## The Thesis Nobody Has Written Yet

Every football game ever made gets the same thing wrong: they simulate football. Madden simulates 22 players. Retro Bowl simulates passing. Blood Bowl simulates a turn-based match. Even the card games — 1st & Goal, Masters of the Gridiron — simulate drives with dice and card comparisons.

**Nobody has built a football game where the football is the LANGUAGE, not the SIMULATION.**

Balatro doesn't simulate poker. You never "play poker against an opponent." Poker hands are the ALPHABET — the building blocks of a mathematical scoring engine. The game is about manipulating that alphabet to produce exponential numbers. Poker players don't recognize Balatro as poker. They recognize it as something that USES poker to create a new feeling.

**TORCH should not simulate football. TORCH should use football as the alphabet for a multiplier-stacking, combo-chaining, math-as-spectacle engine where drives are combos, plays are cards, and touchdowns are the moment the fire animation triggers.**

The gap in the market isn't "football card game." It's "the feeling of watching your 12-play, 80-yard touchdown drive resolve through a cascade of multipliers, badges, and stacked bonuses, with the yard counter ticking up like Balatro's chip counter until the whole screen catches fire."

Nobody has built that. We should.

## What Cuts Across Every Hit

After reading through every relevant game — Balatro, Slay the Spire, Inscryption, Monster Train, Luck Be a Landlord, Shotgun King, Clutchtime, Baseball Highlights 2045, Blood Bowl, Retro Bowl, and more — here are the patterns that separate the great from the forgotten:

### 1. A Familiar Language Turned Alien
Balatro uses poker. Shotgun King uses chess. Inscryption uses sacrifice mechanics everyone knows from MTG. The hook is always: "I already know the pieces, but what the hell is happening to them?" TORCH's language is football — runs, passes, blitzes, touchdowns. Everyone already speaks it.

### 2. Two Numbers Multiplied Together
Balatro: Chips x Mult. This is the simplest possible scoring engine that still produces exponential growth. Two axes of investment. Balanced growth outperforms stacking one dimension. Every modifier is legible: "does this help my chips or my mult?"

### 3. The Constraint That Creates Identity
Balatro's 5-joker limit. StS's 3-energy system. Monster Train's 3 floors. The BEST games don't give you everything — they give you 5 slots and force you to define yourself with them. Your build IS your identity. Cutting is as important as adding.

### 4. The Combo Discovery Moment
"Wait — THESE two things work TOGETHER?!" Dead Branch + Corruption in StS. Baron + Mime in Balatro. The Unkillable totem on wolves in Inscryption. The eureka of finding an emergent synergy the game "didn't intend" (but absolutely did) is the single most retainable moment in any deckbuilder.

### 5. The Score Reveal as Theatre
Balatro's scoring animation — cards stepping forward, jokers activating left-to-right, mult ticking up, fire effects, escalating sound pitch — isn't a UI feature. It IS the game. Strip the animation and Balatro is a spreadsheet. The reveal transforms math into spectacle. Every hit in this genre has an equivalent moment.

### 6. Push-Your-Luck as Skeleton
Quacks of Quedlinburg. Can't Stop. Even Balatro (skip this blind for a tag, or play for money?). The best games make the player ask "do I stop here or push further?" on every meaningful decision. Football's down system is ALREADY this — each down is a push, 4th down is the ultimate gamble.

### 7. Short Runs, High Frequency
Balatro: 30-45 min. StS: 45-60 min. Shotgun King: 15-20 min. Baseball Highlights 2045: 10-15 min per game. The "one more run" factor requires that runs are SHORT enough to contemplate starting another one. TORCH games should be 15-25 minutes.

## What's Missing From Everything That Exists

### Nobody Has Made a "Drive" Feel Like a Combo
In fighting games, a combo is a sequence of connected hits where each hit feeds the next. In Tony Hawk, a combo is a chain of tricks where the multiplier climbs until you land (or bail). In football, a drive is EXACTLY this — a sequence of connected plays where momentum builds until you score (or turn it over). But no football game has TREATED a drive as a combo with mechanical escalation. Plays just... happen sequentially. Yards accumulate linearly.

**The missing design: Each successful play in a drive should mechanically multiply subsequent plays.** First down: base yards. Second first down: base yards x 1.2. Third: x 1.5. A 12-play drive should EXPONENTIALLY outperform two 6-play drives in scoring potential. This makes sustained drives feel like Balatro's fire — the numbers accelerate as you chain.

### Nobody Has Made 4th Down Feel Like Gambling
4th down is the most dramatic moment in football. Analytics says go for it more than coaches do. But in every football game, 4th down is just "another play but scarier." Nobody has given 4th down a MECHANICAL reward beyond the yards gained.

**The missing design: Converting on 4th down should grant a PERMANENT multiplier for the rest of the drive (or the game).** Like Balatro's xMult jokers, 4th-down conversions don't just keep the drive alive — they BREAK your scoring formula. Going for it on 4th & 3 and converting gives you x1.5 on every play for the rest of the drive. Going for it on 4th & 8 and converting gives you x2.0. The risk is proportional to the down-and-distance. Failing gives the opponent field position AND a momentum flip. This is "The Riverboat Gambler" from the inspiration doc, but DEEPER.

### Nobody Has Made the Playbook a Build
In Balatro, your 5 jokers ARE your build. In StS, your deck IS your character. But in football games, playbooks are static menus. You pick from a list of plays someone else designed.

**The missing design: Your playbook IS your roguelike deck.** You draft it. You thin it. You enhance individual plays. You find synergies between plays that were never "intended." A "Shotgun Spread" play card enhanced with "Quick Release" and paired with a WR who has the "Route Runner" badge creates a combo that no playbook in the game explicitly contains. You BUILT that play. It's yours.

### Nobody Has Used the Scoreboard as the Multiplier Display
Balatro shows you the Chips x Mult calculation live. The whole screen IS the math. But football games hide their math behind animations. The scoreboard shows score, time, down & distance — information, not excitement.

**The missing design: The TORCH scoreboard should show your active multipliers in real-time.** Like Balatro's joker row, your current Drive Mult, your active badge combos, your 4th-down conversion buffs — all visible, all ticking, all building toward the MOMENT when you score and everything resolves. The scoreboard IS the engine display.

### Nobody Has Made Defense a "Boss Blind"
In Balatro, Boss Blinds don't just have more HP — they CHANGE THE RULES. The Eye says "no repeating hand types." The Needle says "one hand only." They force you to ADAPT your build. But in football games, defense is just "they try to stop you."

**The missing design: Opposing defenses should be Boss Blinds with constraints.** A "Tampa 2" defense doesn't just lower your passing yards — it REMOVES a card from your hand each play (simulating coverage taking away options). A "Cover 0 Blitz" doesn't just increase sack chance — it gives you ONLY 3 seconds to play your card (literally, a timer) because the rush is coming. The defense changes HOW you play, not just how well you play.

### Nobody Has Made a Football "Score Reveal"
This is the biggest gap. When a play resolves in any football game, you see: "Run for 7 yards." Maybe an animation. That's it. There's no MOMENT. No build-up. No reveal.

**The missing design: The TORCH play resolution should be a 3-5 second theatrical reveal.**

1. You play your cards (play + player + optional torch card)
2. The opponent's defense reveals
3. The base yards appear: **+4**
4. Badge combo fires: **+4 +3** (badge bonus ticks in with a sound)
5. Drive multiplier applies: **(+7) x 1.5** (the mult ticks up)
6. 4th-down buff: **x 2.0** (bigger, deeper sound)
7. Final yards slam onto the field: **21 YARDS** (screen shakes, particles burst)
8. If it's a TD: **EVERYTHING MULTIPLIES** — drive yards x momentum x torch bonus = TORCH POINTS. Fire animation. Bass drop. The number counts up like a slot machine.

This is the game. This 5-second moment is why people play.

## The Opinionated Design: How TORCH Should Work

### Core Formula

```
PLAY SCORE = (Base Yards + Synergy Bonus) x Drive Multiplier x Situation Modifier

TORCH SCORE = Total Drive Yards x Momentum x Fire Bonus
```

Two axes: **Yards** (the chips) and **Multipliers** (the mult). Everything in the game modifies one or both.

### The Drive as a Combo

A drive is a sequence of plays. Each play in the drive has a **Drive Position** (1st play, 2nd play, 3rd play...).

| Drive Position | Drive Multiplier |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Play 1-2 | x1.0 (base) |
| Play 3-4 | x1.2 |
| Play 5-6 | x1.5 |
| Play 7-8 | x1.8 |
| Play 9-10 | x2.0 |
| Play 11+ | x2.5 |

**This is the key insight.** Long, sustained drives don't just move the ball — they MULTIPLY your scoring power. A 12-play touchdown drive might accumulate the same yards as a 3-play scoring drive, but the TORCH score is 2-3x higher because every play was multiplied.

This creates a real strategic tension: Do you throw deep for quick scores (low multiplier, high risk) or grind it out for maximum multiplier (slow, but each play compounds)?

**Stalled drives RESET the multiplier.** Punt = multiplier back to 1.0. Turnover = multiplier FLIPS to the opponent (they start their drive at your accumulated multiplier x 0.5). This makes turnovers devastating AND exciting for the other side — just like a pick-six.

### The Playbook as Roguelike Deck

You start each run (season/tournament) with a **base playbook of 10 play cards** determined by your **Coach Archetype**:

**Coach Archetypes (Like Balatro Decks)**

| Coach | Philosophy | Starting Plays | Special Rule |
|-------|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| **"The Professor"** | Air Raid | 7 pass, 3 run. Heavy short/quick. | Completions chain: consecutive completions add +1 Base Yards each. |
| **"The Groundhog"** | Smashmouth | 7 run, 3 pass. Power and option. | First Downs from runs grant +0.1 permanent Drive Mult for the rest of the game. |
| **"The Gambler"** | Aggressive/Trick | 5 pass, 3 run, 2 trick plays. | 4th down conversions grant x1.5 Drive Mult. Punting resets mult to 0.5 (below base). |
| **"The Architect"** | West Coast | 5 short pass, 3 run, 2 play-action. | Play-action after a run: x2 to next play's yards. Rewards sequencing. |
| **"The Blitz King"** | Defense-First | 4 pass, 4 run, 2 defensive boost cards. | Turnovers give your offense a x1.5 head start on drive mult. |

Each coach fundamentally changes what cards are strong, what synergies are available, and how you approach every drive. Just like Balatro decks.

### Play Cards: Dual-Use Design (The Neon White Principle)

Every play card has TWO uses:

**SNAP** (Execute the play): Gain the play's Base Yards, trigger synergies.
**BURN** (Sacrifice the card): Don't gain yards, but gain a **resource** instead.

| Burn Resource | What It Does |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Momentum** | +0.2 to current Drive Multiplier |
| **Audible** | See the opponent's next defensive card before playing |
| **Timeout** | Freeze the 2-minute drill clock for 1 play |
| **Film Study** | Next play of this TYPE gets +50% Base Yards |

This creates agonizing decisions every hand. You drew "HB Dive" and "Four Verts" and "PA Crosser." You need 8 yards for a first down. Do you SNAP the PA Crosser for a safe 6-8 yards? Or do you BURN the HB Dive for Momentum (+0.2 mult), then SNAP Four Verts with the boosted multiplier for a potential 15+ yard explosive play?

The burn is never obvious. It depends on your drive state, your multiplier, your remaining deck, the defensive look. This is the "Every card is a strategic choice" principle from Neon White.

### Player Cards: The Joker Slots

You carry **4 active player cards** per side (offense/defense). These are your Balatro jokers — passive modifiers that trigger under specific conditions.

| Player | Badge | Effect (When It Fires) |
|--------|-------|----------------------|
| Sampson (WR) | SPEED_LINES | Deep passes: +4 yards, +20 TORCH pts |
| Vasquez (SLOT) | FLAME | 3rd/4th down: +3 yards, x1.2 Drive Mult |
| Walsh (RB) | BRICK | Power runs, QB sneaks: +3 yards, +15 TORCH pts |
| Avery (QB) | FOOTBALL | Deep passes: +3 yards, x1.1 to all pass plays |

Players fire LEFT TO RIGHT — just like Balatro jokers. This means the ORDER you arrange your roster matters.

**Example:** Avery (FOOTBALL: x1.1 to all passes) is in slot 1. Sampson (SPEED_LINES: +4 yards on deep passes) is in slot 2. You call "Four Verts" (deep pass, base 12 yards).

- Base: 12 yards
- Avery fires: 12 x 1.1 = 13.2 → round to 13
- Sampson fires: 13 + 4 = 17 yards
- Drive Mult (play 7): x1.8
- **Final: 17 x 1.8 = 30.6 yards. 30-yard bomb.**

Swap the order (Sampson first, Avery second): 12 + 4 = 16, then 16 x 1.1 = 17.6. Same players, different order, different result. The Balatro ordering puzzle, in football.

### Torch Cards: The Spectral/Tarot Layer

Torch Cards are rare, powerful, one-use modifiers. Collected between games (from the Booster Shop, as drive rewards, from achievements).

Categories:
- **Pre-Snap** (play before snapping — like Balatro Tarots): Modify the upcoming play
- **Reactive** (play after seeing the result — like Balatro Spectrals): Change reality after the fact

| Torch Card | Type | Effect |
|-----------|------|--------|
| **Hot Hand** | Pre-Snap | Next play's Drive Mult is DOUBLED |
| **Film Room** | Pre-Snap | See the defensive card before choosing your play |
| **Challenge Flag** | Reactive | Contest a turnover: 50% chance to overturn |
| **Hail Mary** | Pre-Snap | This play's Base Yards become 0-50 (uniform random). Pure gamble. |
| **No Huddle** | Pre-Snap | Play 2 cards this snap instead of 1. Both resolve. Both chain. |
| **Red Zone Dagger** | Pre-Snap | Inside the 20: This play's Base Yards x3. Outside the 20: does nothing. |
| **Steal Their Signs** | Pre-Snap | Opponent's defense plays face-up for the next 3 snaps |
| **Ice the Kicker** | Reactive | Opponent's field goal attempt: 40% chance to miss |

Torch Cards are the SPECTACLE layer. They're rare, dramatic, and game-defining. When someone plays "Hot Hand" on play 9 of a drive (drive mult x2.0, now DOUBLED to x4.0) and then snaps a "Four Verts" with Sampson's SPEED_LINES badge... that's the Balatro fire animation moment. That's the clip people share.

### Defense: Boss Blinds, Not Stat Comparisons

The opponent's defense doesn't just "reduce yards." Each defensive scheme is a CONSTRAINT that changes how you play.

| Defensive Scheme | Constraint |
|-----------------|-----------|
| **Cover 0 Blitz** | You have 3 seconds to play your card (timer). Sack rate doubles. BUT if you beat it, +50% yards (nobody's home). |
| **Tampa 2** | Your top-2 highest-yard pass plays are DISABLED this snap. Must use your weaker options. |
| **Spy** | If you repeat a play type from last snap, the defense counters automatically (0 yards + turnover risk). |
| **Goal Line Stack** | Run plays capped at 3 yards max. But pass plays are uncapped (all-in on the run stop). |
| **Prevent** | Deep passes limited to 15 yards max. Short passes and runs are unmodified. Time-killing scheme. |
| **Disguise** | Defense card is HIDDEN until after you snap. You're guessing. Removes information advantage. |

This is the Boss Blind principle. Tampa 2 is "The Plant" (face cards debuffed). Cover 0 is "The Needle" (time pressure). Spy is "The Eye" (can't repeat). Each one FORCES you to adapt your playbook to the constraint, not just play your best card every time.

### The Between-Games Loop: The Shop

After each game in a run (season/tournament), you visit the **Locker Room**:

1. **Draft a Play** (choose 1 of 3 random play cards to add to your playbook)
2. **Scout a Player** (choose 1 of 3 random player cards — may replace a starter)
3. **Buy from the Pro Shop** (spend TORCH points on Torch Cards, play enhancements, or playbook trimming)
4. **Cut a Play** (remove a play card from your playbook — costs increase each cut, like StS card removal)

The economy:
- **TORCH Points** earned per game based on performance (yards, TDs, multiplier peaks, badge combos fired)
- TORCH Points spent in the Pro Shop
- Unspent TORCH Points earn **interest** (Balatro-style: +1 per 10 held, cap of 5)
- This creates the spend-vs-save tension: buy now or bank for a bigger purchase later?

### The Run Structure: A 6-Game Season

A single TORCH run is a **6-game season**:

| Game | Opponent | Difficulty | Special Rule |
|------|----------|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | Weak team | Easy | None (tutorial opponent) |
| 2 | Average team | Medium | One defensive constraint |
| 3 | **Rival** (mini-boss) | Hard | Two constraints + team-specific rule |
| 4 | Average team | Medium | Shop has better options |
| 5 | Strong team | Hard | Three constraints |
| 6 | **Championship** (boss) | Very Hard | Unique boss rule + all previous constraints |

Between every game: Locker Room (shop/draft/cut).

After Game 3 (the rival): **Halftime Show** — special one-time shop with powerful options, bonus TORCH point payout, potential to trade a player or acquire a rare Torch Card.

A run takes **~20-25 minutes** (4 min per game + 1 min per shop visit). Short enough for "one more run." Long enough to build a satisfying deck.

### Meta-Progression: Unlock Options, Not Power

Between runs, permanent unlocks expand your OPTIONS:

| Unlock Type | Example |
|------------|---------|
| New Coach | Win with The Professor → unlock The Gambler |
| New Plays | Win 3 games with Air Raid plays → unlock "Flea Flicker" |
| New Players | Score 500 TORCH pts in one run → unlock new WR archetype |
| New Torch Cards | Convert on 4th down 10 times → unlock "Gutsy Call" card |
| New Difficulty | Beat the Championship → unlock "All-Pro" modifier (harder ante scaling) |

All unlocks add OPTIONS, not POWER. A new coach isn't better — it's different. A new play isn't stronger — it enables new synergies. A fresh account with a skilled player can beat the championship. An experienced account has more VARIETY.

### Difficulty Scaling: Ascension, Not Sliders

Like StS's Ascension system, TORCH has stacking difficulty modifiers:

| Tier | Modifier |
|------|---------|
| Rookie | Base difficulty |
| Varsity | Opponents call better plays, start with 1 constraint |
| All-Conference | Start with a "dead play" card in your playbook (a curse) |
| All-American | Shop prices +25%, opponents get +1 Drive Mult start |
| Heisman | Boss games have 2 unique constraints instead of 1 |
| Natty | All modifiers stacked. The ultimate challenge. |

Each tier is its own achievement. Beating Natty with all 5 coaches = 30 completion targets = hundreds of hours of gameplay. Same content, infinite replayability.

## The Scoring Moment: Where TORCH Lives or Dies

Everything above is scaffolding for ONE moment: **the play resolution.**

Here's what it should feel like:

```
You're on play 8 of a drive. Drive Mult is x1.8.
You converted on 4th-and-6 two plays ago: +x1.5 4th-down buff.
Your total active mult: x1.8 x 1.5 = x2.7.

You play "PA Post" (base 10 yards).
Avery (FOOTBALL badge) is in slot 1: x1.1 to passes.
Sampson (SPEED_LINES badge) is in slot 2: +4 yards on deep passes.

The defense reveals: Cover 3 (no special constraint — clean look).

RESOLUTION ANIMATION:
1. Cards slam together — spark burst impact
2. "PA POST" text appears — base yards tick in: +10
3. Avery badge lights up — "+10 → x1.1 → 11" (quick tick sound)
4. Sampson badge lights up — "11 + 4 → 15" (higher pitch tick)
5. Drive Mult bar pulses — "15 x 2.7" (the mult ROLLS UP: 2.0... 2.3... 2.5... 2.7)
6. 4th-Down Buff icon FIRES — sound deepens, screen tints gold
7. Final yards SLAM: "40 YARDS" — screen shakes, particles burst
8. TOUCHDOWN — full celebration: fire animation, bass drop, TORCH points
   cascade upward like a jackpot

TORCH SCORE for this drive:
  87 total yards x 2.7 final mult x 1.5 TD bonus = 352 TORCH POINTS
  The counter rolls from 0 to 352 with escalating pitch.
  The flame effect intensifies as the number climbs.
```

**THIS IS THE GAME.** Everything else exists to make this moment happen more frequently and more intensely.

## What to Build First

The minimum viable "does this feel good" prototype:

1. **One drive.** 10 play cards, 4 player badges, drive multiplier that climbs.
2. **One resolution animation.** Base yards + badge bonus + drive mult = final yards, animated step-by-step with sound.
3. **One 4th-down decision.** Go for it or punt. Converting gives x1.5 mult.
4. **One defensive constraint.** The defense removes your best card from hand.
5. **One touchdown moment.** Full cascade: drive yards x momentum x fire bonus = TORCH score. Particles. Sound. Shake.

If that FEELS right — if watching the multiplier build and the score resolve gives you the Balatro dopamine hit — then everything else is iteration.

If it doesn't, no amount of features will save it.

**Build the feeling first. Build the game around the feeling.**
