# Sports UX Research: Retro & Modern Arcade Vibe

## 1. Representing Complex Stats Simply
*   **The "Core Four":** Both *Retro Bowl* and *Tecmo Bowl* prove that you only need 4-5 core attributes (Speed, Strength, Stamina, Accuracy) to define a player's identity. Anything more risks "UI Bloat."
*   **Visual Shorthand:** Use OVR (Overall) as the primary hook for roster building, but allow players to see the "Bar" or "Stars" for specific attributes immediately. Avoid hidden spreadsheets.
*   **Stamina as Decay:** Simplify fatigue. Stamina isn't just "energy"—it's a performance decay curve. A tired QB loses accuracy; a tired RB loses breakaway speed.
*   **Condition States:** *Tecmo Super Bowl*'s "Condition" (Excellent, Good, Bad) adds a layer of "management feel" without requiring a deep dive into medical stats.
*   **Hidden "Juice":** Dynamic difficulty should feel like the AI "waking up" rather than just making the player slower.

## 2. Pacing of Play-by-Play
*   **The "Gap Time" Philosophy:** Games must fit into the 3–5 minute window (coffee breaks, transit). 
*   **Offense-Centric Pacing:** *Retro Bowl* succeeds by simulating defense. This keeps the player in the high-engagement "Action Seat" 100% of the time.
*   **Zero-Downtime Transitions:** The transition from a whistle to the play-selection screen should be sub-one second. 
*   **Cinematic "Stings":** Brief (1-2 second) pixel-art cutscenes for sacks, diving catches, or touchdowns provide a "visual reward" that breaks the overhead monotony.
*   **No Penalties:** Arcade games feel faster because they remove the "interruptions" of officiating (holding, offsides). Let the game flow.

## 3. What Makes Them Feel "Alive" on Mobile
*   **Affordance-Based Controls:** Avoid the "Virtual Joystick" trap. Use touch-native gestures:
    *   *Pull-back to aim* (Retro Bowl)
    *   *Swipe to juke*
    *   *Tap repeatedly to "power through"* (Tecmo Bowl)
*   **Physicality & Impact:** 
    *   **Screen Shake:** A subtle shake on a big hit makes a pixel tackle feel heavy.
    *   **Haptics:** Short, sharp vibration on a catch; a lingering rumble on a sack.
*   **Visual "Pop":**
    *   **Breaking the Frame:** Card art or UI elements that overlap borders create a premium, collectible feel (Madden Mobile).
    *   **High Contrast:** Use matte backgrounds with neon/vibrant action colors to make the field "glow" on OLED screens.
*   **The "Front Office" Vibe:** The management side (Drafting, Morale, Facility Upgrades) should feel like a simple "Tycoon" game. It gives the arcade action a purpose.
*   **Dynamic Soundscapes:** Crowds that get louder on 3rd down or during a goal-line stand make the small screen feel like a stadium.

## 4. Anti-Patterns to Avoid (Warnings from Madden Mobile)
*   **Currency Overload:** When the game has 5+ currencies, players spend more time in menus than on the field.
*   **Sensory Overload UI:** Avoid flashing buttons and nested tabs. If a player can't find the "Play" button in 2 seconds, the UX has failed.
*   **Stat Inflation:** If a player has "125 Speed," the number becomes meaningless. Keep the scale grounded (e.g., 1-20 or 1-100).
