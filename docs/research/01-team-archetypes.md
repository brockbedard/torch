# TORCH Team Archetypes Research
## 4 Fictional College Football Teams

*Research Date: 2026-03-22*

---

## Design Philosophy

These four teams are built around a rock-paper-scissors dynamic where no team is strictly dominant. Each offense exploits a specific defensive weakness, and each defense shuts down a specific offensive style. The result is that matchup selection creates meaningful strategic tension before a single card is played.

### Counter-Play Matrix

| | vs Sentinels DEF (Press Man) | vs Wolves DEF (Zone Read) | vs Stags DEF (Swarm Blitz) | vs Serpents DEF (Pattern Match) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sentinels OFF (Run & Shoot)** | Mirror | STRONG (option routes vs zone) | WEAK (no time to read) | Neutral |
| **Wolves OFF (Triple Option)** | STRONG (can't man-cover pitch) | Mirror | Neutral | WEAK (LBs read mesh) |
| **Stags OFF (Spread RPO)** | Neutral | WEAK (zone reads the RPO) | Mirror | STRONG (RPO beats pattern match) |
| **Serpents OFF (Air Raid)** | WEAK (press jams timing) | Neutral | STRONG (quick passes beat blitz) | Mirror |

**Why this works:** Each team has one offense-vs-defense matchup where they dominate (+), one where they struggle (-), and two that are roughly even. No team auto-wins.

## TEAM 1: The Crimson Sentinels

### Identity
- **School:** Ridgemont University
- **Mascot:** Sentinels (armored guardian figure, shield and spear motif)
- **Colors:** `#8B0000` Crimson / `#C4A265` Antique Gold
- **Motto:** *"Eyes Up, Hands Ready"*
- **Personality/Vibe:** Disciplined. Adaptive. The chess team that also bench-presses 315. Ridgemont is the program that doesn't beat you with raw talent -- they beat you because their receivers made the right read and their QB put the ball where only his guy could get it. Crowds are quiet until a big play, then deafening. Old-money program energy, but earned respect, not entitlement. Think: a program that has won 9 games every year for 15 years but never quite gets the playoff bid.

### Offense: Run & Shoot
**Philosophy:** Four-wide, one-back formation. Receivers read the defense mid-route and adjust. The QB and WRs must be on the same page -- if the corner plays inside leverage, the WR breaks outside. If the safety cheats, the route bends underneath. It is not a pure air-raid volume attack; it is a precision system where every route has 2-3 possible breaks based on the coverage shell.

**Real-world inspiration:** Houston Cougars under Jack Pardee/Andre Ware (1989), June Jones' Hawaii Warriors. The system thrives on receiver intelligence and QB anticipation. It is inherently anti-man-coverage because option routes create natural separation against man defenders who commit to a single break point.

**Why it plays differently from Air Raid:** Air Raid is a volume system (throw it 50+ times, mesh/shallow cross, same routes regardless of coverage). Run & Shoot is a reactive system -- fewer concepts but each concept has built-in answers to every coverage. Fewer total passes, but higher completion rate and more explosive plays when the read is right.

**Offensive Scheme Tags:** `option_routes`, `four_wide`, `sight_adjust`, `choice_routes`

#### 7v7 Defensive Roster: Press Man / Cover 1

**Philosophy:** Get in receivers' faces at the line. Jam. Disrupt timing. One deep safety as the last line. Corners play press-man, safeties play robber/spy roles. The idea: if you can't release clean, the whole timing-based offense collapses. Extremely aggressive at the point of attack but vulnerable to misdirection and option-pitch concepts that exploit man defenders' inability to pass off assignments.

**Real-world inspiration:** Pat Narduzzi's Michigan State defenses, early-2010s Seattle Seahawks "Legion of Boom" press-man principles applied to college speed.

**Defensive Scheme Tags:** `press_man`, `cover_1`, `jam_release`, `trail_technique`

## TEAM 2: The Timber Wolves

### Identity
- **School:** Northern Pines A&M
- **Mascot:** Timber Wolves (snarling wolf head, pine tree collar detail)
- **Colors:** `#1B3A2D` Forest Green / `#D4D4D4` Silver
- **Motto:** *"Run Through Them"*
- **Personality/Vibe:** Blue-collar. Relentless. The team nobody wants to play in November. Northern Pines recruits linemen from logging towns and fullbacks who think a 3-yard gain is a moral victory. Their fans stand the entire game. The band plays the fight song after first downs, not touchdowns, because first downs are what this program values. Opponents leave the field sore. There is no glamour here -- just execution, conditioning, and the belief that the 4th quarter belongs to whoever wants it more.

### Offense: Triple Option (Flexbone)
**Philosophy:** Under center. Fullback dive is option one. QB keep is option two. Pitch to the slotback is option three. The offense reads defensive linemen rather than blocking them -- "Why block a guy when you can just run away from him?" -- leaving unblocked defenders frozen by the mesh point while the ball goes where they aren't. Ball-control, clock-killing, ground-based attack. Passing is rare (15-20% of plays) but devastating when the safeties creep up.

**Real-world inspiration:** Army/Navy/Georgia Tech under Paul Johnson. The service academies perfected this system because it allows physically smaller teams to compete with superior athletes by using scheme leverage and option reads to neutralize athletic advantages.

**Why it plays differently from Power Run:** Power run is about blocking everyone and running downhill behind a lead blocker. Triple option is about NOT blocking key defenders and instead reading them -- it is a decision-based system, not a brute-force system. Power run wants 4 yards. Triple option wants to hit the one gap that the defense gives you, which might be 2 yards or might be 25.

**Offensive Scheme Tags:** `flexbone`, `triple_option`, `fullback_dive`, `pitch_read`, `midline`, `clock_control`

#### 7v7 Defensive Roster: Zone Read Defense / Scrape Exchange

**Philosophy:** Gap-sound, assignment football. Every defender has a gap and they fill it. The key is "spill and rally" -- force everything to bounce outside where pursuit angles bring it down. Linebackers read mesh points, defensive ends squeeze. Safeties play force. Nobody freelances. The defense wins by being exactly where they are supposed to be on every play and making the offense earn every yard between the tackles.

**Real-world inspiration:** Wisconsin / Iowa "bend but don't break" zone defenses. Cover 3 shell with a walked-up safety as the extra run defender. Everything funnels to the sideline where the defense has numbers.

**Defensive Scheme Tags:** `gap_sound`, `cover_3`, `spill_rally`, `force_player`, `run_fit`

## TEAM 3: The Stags

### Identity
- **School:** Crestview College
- **Mascot:** Stags (leaping stag with full antler rack, aggressive forward pose)
- **Colors:** `#F28C28` Burnt Orange / `#1C1C1C` Charcoal Black
- **Motto:** *"Strike First, Strike Fast"*
- **Personality/Vibe:** Explosive. Electric. The team that sells out 40,000 seats because something insane happens every game. Crestview plays fast, recruits athletes over position players, and believes that if you score in under 2 minutes, the other team can't keep up. Their coach is a hoodie-and-visor type who looks like he sleeps in the film room. The crowd brings cowbells. The stadium is loud. The offense is louder. This is the team that either wins 52-48 or loses 52-48 -- there is no middle ground.

### Offense: Spread RPO
**Philosophy:** Shotgun, 3-4 wide, run-pass option on nearly every play. The QB reads a single defender (usually the MIKE or a DE) and decides in real time whether to hand off the inside zone run, pull and throw a quick pass to the flat/slant, or (on some plays) keep and run. The offense forces the defense to be wrong -- if the LB steps up to stop the run, the pass is open. If the LB drops to cover the pass, the run lane opens. It is a numbers game played at tempo.

**Real-world inspiration:** Lincoln Riley's Oklahoma offense (2017-2021), early Chip Kelly Oregon Ducks. The RPO revolution that changed college football in the 2010s by exploiting the NCAA's generous 3-yard downfield rule for offensive linemen.

**Why it plays differently from Run & Shoot:** Run & Shoot is a pure passing system with built-in route adjustments. Spread RPO is a dual-threat system where the run game is real and the pass game exists to punish defenders who commit to the run. The QB's read is pre-snap or first-step, not mid-route. Tempo and simplicity over complexity.

**Offensive Scheme Tags:** `spread`, `rpo`, `inside_zone`, `bubble_screen`, `tempo`, `dual_threat`

#### 7v7 Defensive Roster: Swarm Blitz / Pressure Defense

**Philosophy:** Create chaos. Blitz from everywhere. The goal is not to read the offense but to hit the QB before he can execute. Run exotic blitz packages with five or six rushers and play Cover 0 or Cover 1 behind it. When it works, it is suffocating -- sacks, turnovers, three-and-outs. When it doesn't, there are guys running wide open. This defense lives on the edge. It is a high-risk, high-reward identity that creates turnovers and short fields for the offense.

**Real-world inspiration:** Early 2010s Mississippi State under Dan Mullen (3-3-5 pressure), Brent Venables' Clemson defenses (exotic blitz packages from multiple fronts). The philosophy: make the QB uncomfortable and the protection scheme collapse.

**Defensive Scheme Tags:** `blitz_heavy`, `cover_0`, `exotic_pressure`, `edge_rush`, `chaos`

Also struggles with any offense that uses quick passes to exploit vacated zones.

## TEAM 4: The Serpents

### Identity
- **School:** Blackwater State
- **Mascot:** Serpents (coiled king cobra, hood flared, fangs visible)
- **Colors:** `#2E0854` Deep Purple / `#39FF14` Venom Green
- **Motto:** *"Death by a Thousand Cuts"*
- **Personality/Vibe:** Cerebral. Methodical. Ruthless. Blackwater State is the program that other coaches stay up at night game-planning for because nothing is ever what it looks like. The pre-snap motion reveals nothing. The coverage shell shifts after the snap. The offense throws 8-yard completions on 14 consecutive plays and suddenly it is 28-0. Their fans are smug. Their stadium is called "The Pit." Their HC wears a tie on the sideline and never raises his voice. Opponents describe playing Blackwater as "death by paper cuts -- you don't realize you're bleeding until it is too late."

### Offense: Air Raid
**Philosophy:** Shotgun, four-wide, one-back. Throw the ball 60% of the time. The same 4-5 concepts (mesh, Y-cross, shallow cross, four verticals, stick) run over and over from slightly different formations and motions. The defense knows what is coming but cannot stop it because the spacing is perfect and the ball comes out in under 2.5 seconds. No trick plays. No misdirection. Just relentless, precise passing that exploits every coverage weakness through volume and timing.

**Real-world inspiration:** Mike Leach's Washington State / Texas Tech offenses. The purest air raid: limited playbook, unlimited reps. The QB throws 45-55 times a game. The system makes average QBs look good and good QBs look elite because the reads are simple and the windows are schemed open.

**Why it plays differently from Run & Shoot:** Air Raid routes are fixed -- the WR runs the same route regardless of coverage, and the QB reads the defense to find the open man. Run & Shoot routes are variable -- the WR adjusts his route based on coverage. Air Raid is a "read the defense" system; Run & Shoot is a "react to the defense" system. Air Raid is higher volume, lower risk per throw. Run & Shoot is lower volume, higher reward per throw.

**Offensive Scheme Tags:** `air_raid`, `mesh`, `shallow_cross`, `four_verticals`, `quick_game`, `volume_passing`

#### 7v7 Defensive Roster: Pattern-Matching / Disguise Defense

**Philosophy:** Show one thing, play another. Line up in a Cover 2 shell, rotate to Cover 3 at the snap. Show blitz, drop into zone. The goal is to make the QB wrong on his pre-snap read so that his first throw is into coverage. Defenders play "match" rules -- they start in zone, but when a receiver enters their area, they lock on and play man. It is the most complex defensive system to execute but the most difficult to attack because the offense cannot predict what it will see.

**Real-world inspiration:** Nick Saban's Alabama defenses (pattern-matching Cover 3, MABLE/MOFO shell rotations), Kirby Smart's Georgia defenses. The philosophy of "make simple concepts for the offense look complicated" by disguising the coverage shell.

**Defensive Scheme Tags:** `pattern_match`, `disguise`, `shell_rotation`, `match_zone`, `quarters`

## Rock-Paper-Scissors Summary

```
Sentinels (Run & Shoot + Press Man)
  Offense STRONG vs: Wolves defense (option routes torch zone)
  Offense WEAK vs:   Stags defense (blitz kills route development)
  Defense STRONG vs: Serpents offense (press jams air raid timing)
  Defense WEAK vs:   Wolves offense (can't man-cover the pitch)

Wolves (Triple Option + Zone Read Defense)
  Offense STRONG vs: Sentinels defense (option washes man coverage)
  Offense WEAK vs:   Serpents defense (pattern-match reads the mesh)
  Defense STRONG vs: Stags offense (zone reads RPO, doesn't bite)
  Defense WEAK vs:   Sentinels offense (option routes exploit zone seams)

Stags (Spread RPO + Swarm Blitz)
  Offense STRONG vs: Serpents defense (RPO attacks before disguise resolves)
  Offense WEAK vs:   Wolves defense (zone discipline shuts down RPO)
  Defense STRONG vs: Wolves offense (blitz blows up option mesh)
  Defense WEAK vs:   Serpents offense (quick passes beat the blitz)

Serpents (Air Raid + Pattern Match)
  Offense STRONG vs: Stags defense (quick release beats blitz every time)
  Offense WEAK vs:   Sentinels defense (press man disrupts timing routes)
  Defense STRONG vs: Wolves offense (pattern-match reads the triple option)
  Defense WEAK vs:   Stags offense (RPO exploits pattern-match sorting time)
```

### The Circular Dynamic
```
Sentinels → beat Serpents → beat Stags → beat Wolves → beat Sentinels
```
This is a clean 4-way circle where each team has a natural prey and a natural predator. When two teams with neutral matchups meet (e.g., Sentinels vs Stags), the game comes down to player quality, card play, and in-game adjustments -- which is exactly where TORCH's card-based gameplay shines.

## Roster Design Notes for TORCH Implementation

### Format Compatibility
The existing game uses 4 starters + 2 bench per side (offense and defense). The rosters above are designed for a 7v7 expanded format (1 QB + 3 OL + 3 skill on offense, 7 on defense). If the game retains the current 4+2 format, the rosters can be trimmed:

**Offense (4+2 format):** Keep QB + 3 skill players as starters, move 1 skill + 1 OL to bench, drop remaining OL (they become implicit/abstracted).

**Defense (4+2 format):** Keep the 4 highest-OVR defenders as starters, 2 remaining as bench, drop the 7th.

### OVR Distribution
Each team follows the same distribution pattern as existing teams (Canyon Tech, Iron Ridge):
- 1 star player at 84 OVR (the "face of the program")
- 1 QB at 78-80 OVR
- Supporting players range 70-80 OVR
- Total offensive OVR is roughly equivalent across teams (~530-540 for 7 players)
- Total defensive OVR is roughly equivalent across teams (~530-540 for 7 players)

### Badge Distribution
Badges are assigned to match scheme identity:
- **Sentinels:** Heavy on CROSSHAIR (quick passes), SPEED_LINES (deep threat), GLOVE (catches) offense; PADLOCK (man coverage) defense
- **Wolves:** Heavy on HELMET (power running), BRICK (blocking), CLIPBOARD (option plays) offense; HELMET + EYE (read-and-react) defense
- **Stags:** Heavy on FLAME (clutch), CLEAT (screens/RPO), BOLT (quick game) offense; SPEED_LINES (blitz) defense
- **Serpents:** Heavy on GLOVE (possession catching), CROSSHAIR (accuracy) offense; CLIPBOARD (scheme/disguise), EYE (coverage reads) defense
