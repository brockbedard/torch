# The Ember Eight — Conference & Team Identity Bible

**Status:** Design locked. April 10-11, 2026.
**Companion to:** TORCH-V2-SPEC.md (system design).
**Purpose:** This document captures all conference lore, team identities, coaching ghosts, and narrative design. It is the source of truth for world-building.

---

## The Conference

**Name:** The Ember Eight
**Founded:** December 2025 (Phoenix Summit)
**Inaugural season:** Fall 2026 — the season the player plays
**Identity:** The Leftovers. Eight programs nobody else wanted, bound together by shared exclusion from CFB realignment.

### Founding Timeline

- **Spring 2025:** Vermont's athletic director publishes The Letter — an open manifesto in The Athletic calling for a new conference for programs being squeezed out by realignment.
- **Summer-Fall 2025:** Seven other programs respond. Conversations begin.
- **December 2025:** The Phoenix Summit. Athletic directors of all 8 programs meet in Phoenix for three days. The founding charter of the Ember Eight is signed.
- **January 2026:** Wally Reinhardt unexpectedly retires from Larkspur after 30 seasons. Larkspur hires the player as new head coach.
- **Spring-Summer 2026:** All 8 programs prepare for the inaugural season. Schedules finalized. Traditions invented. Nobody knows what to expect.
- **Fall 2026:** The first ever Ember Eight season begins. The player's first game is week 1.

### Conference Identity

The Ember Eight is a conference of misfits. Each program has its own answer to "why didn't anyone want you?" — too small, too remote, too weird, too poor, too proud, too independent, too new. The conference formed because nobody else would have them, and it survives because they refuse to die.

The name comes from the metaphor: an ember is what survives when everything else has burned down. The TORCH brand is the tool you carry; the Ember Eight is the collective fire kept alive across programs.

The conference is brand new and unproven. Every game in the inaugural season matters because the conference itself is on trial. If the Ember Eight doesn't establish itself as a real league, it could collapse.

### The Player Is the Coach

The player names themselves, creates a coach avatar, and takes over as head coach of whichever team they pick. Coach stats are tracked but don't progress in v1. The end-of-season hook is "imagine if your coach could grow" — the v2 multi-season career tease. The first 90 seconds of the game (coach creator + team pick + program history) frame the entire experience.

## Talent Tier System

**Structure:** 2 top / 4 middle / 2 bottom. Power 5 conference shape.

**How tiers manifest:** Starting sub-attribute distribution (not stamps, not anything else).
- **Top tier:** Average roster sub-attributes ~70 (high 4-star average)
- **Middle tier:** Average roster sub-attributes ~52 (3-star average)
- **Bottom tier:** Average roster sub-attributes ~35 (low 2-3 star average)

**Universal rules:**
- Every player starts with exactly 1 stamp (their trait promoted). No tier-based stamp bonuses.
- Soft cap: each player can grow ~20 sub-attribute points across a season.
- Maxed players convert excess helmet stickers to team TORCH points.

**Expected win probabilities:**
- Top vs top: ~50/50
- Top vs middle: ~70/30
- Top vs bottom: ~85/15
- Middle vs middle: ~50/50
- Middle vs bottom: ~70/30
- Bottom vs bottom: ~50/50

**Tier assignments (tentative, not formally locked):**
- Top: Larkspur, Hollowridge
- Middle: Vermont, Helix, Coral Bay, Blackwater
- Bottom: Ridgemont, Sacramento

## The Eight Teams

### 1. Ridgemont Boars

**Colors:** Red
**Region:** Mid-South / Ozarks, Arkansas
**School:** Ridgemont University
**Town:** Ridgemont, Arkansas. Former mill town on a river in a holler in the Ozarks. ~4,000 people. Tyson plant 30 minutes away. WPA-era limestone stadium. Surrounded by hills full of feral hogs.
**Program identity:** Football factory in a tiny Ozark town. The team is the town's only claim to relevance.
**Talent tier:** Bottom

**Ghost: Coach Wendell Pace (1944-2017)**
- Born in St. Louis, Missouri. Father was a high school music teacher who moved the family to Ridgemont in 1955 to teach choral music at the university.
- Played defensive end for the Boars 1962-1965. Captained the defense his senior year.
- Worked for the state highway department for six years. Coached local Pop Warner on weekends. Took a graduate assistant position at Ridgemont in 1972.
- Head coach 1979-2004. 26 seasons. Won 8 conference titles. Made the national title game once, in 1991. Lost it.
- Famous for: keeping a notebook of every play his teams ran, reading three newspapers a day, hating practice (he often cut practice short), being deeply Catholic in a region that mostly wasn't, and writing a weekly column for the Ridgemont Gazette about local history that had nothing to do with football.
- Married once. Wife was a librarian. Two daughters, both became doctors.
- Retired suddenly in 2004 after a heart scare. Spent retirement restoring a farmhouse and writing an unpublished book about the geology of the Ozarks.
- Died in 2017 of complications from a stroke.
- Statue outside the stadium. Players touch his hand on the way out of the tunnel — a tradition that started after his death that he would have hated.

**Current arc:** "Failing to be Pace." 20 years of successors trying to recreate him and failing. The program is haunted by personal failure, not tragedy. Picking the Boars means "I am the coach who finally breaks the curse."

### 2. Vermont Maples

**Colors:** Maple red, gold, dark wood brown
**Region:** Northeast Kingdom, Vermont
**School:** University of Vermont, St. Marlowe campus
**Town:** St. Marlowe, Vermont. Fictional town in the Northeast Kingdom. ~6,000-8,000 people. Old stone university. 25,000-seat stadium in the woods. Maple trees everywhere. River through town. One main street with a town green.
**Program identity:** Old, ivy-adjacent, principled, smug, intellectual. Stanford crossed with Ivy League. Wins through preparation and intellect. Holds itself to standards nobody asked for.
**Talent tier:** Middle

**Ghost: Coach Russell Hadley (1909-1991)**
- Head coach 1956-1979. Played for Vermont in the 1930s, came back to coach after WWII.
- First college coach in America to use film study as a primary tool, not a supplement. Built a film room in the stadium basement in 1961 that's still in use.
- Won 8 conference titles. Refused multiple NFL head coaching offers because he didn't want to leave Vermont.
- Died in 1991.

**Current arc:** "Honor the lineage and carry the conference." Vermont wrote The Letter that founded the Ember Eight. Their AD started this. Vermont's year-one performance equals the league's legitimacy. Picking Vermont means "I'm inheriting a 100-year tradition and the conference I founded needs to justify itself through my performance."

### 3. Hollowridge Spectres

**Colors:** Ice blue
**Region:** Allegheny Mountains, West Virginia
**School:** Hollowridge State University
**Town:** Hollowridge, West Virginia. Small Appalachian town, ~6,000-12,000 people now (was ~18,000 before the flood). The football stadium is on high ground above the destroyed riverside downtown. Fog fills the hollow between the ridges.
**Program identity:** Large public state school (~20,000+ students). Football is the school's national identity. Academically not great — a football factory. Marshall analog.
**Talent tier:** Top

**The Flood of November 1989:**
- A stalled autumn storm system dumped 8+ inches of rain on the Mire River watershed over two days. The river crested at nearly 19 feet above flood stage.
- 43 people died, mostly elderly residents who couldn't evacuate. ~1,200 homes destroyed or damaged beyond repair.
- The historic downtown along the river was destroyed and never rebuilt — now a memorial park with foundations, empty lots, and a small monument to the 43 dead.
- The old hospital was flooded and never reopened (nearest hospital now 45 minutes away).
- Two bridges washed out — one replaced with a smaller one, one never replaced.
- Population dropped from ~18,000 to ~11,000 between 1989 and 2005.
- The football game scheduled for that weekend was canceled and never made up.

**Ghost: Coach Danny Vecchio (1948-2023)**
- Born in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. Italian-American family, third-generation steel mill workers.
- Played defensive end at University of Pittsburgh 1966-1969. Brief NFL tryout, didn't make it.
- Hired as Hollowridge State's defensive coordinator in 1992, three years after the flood. The town was still raw. The program was in disarray. He came in cold, saw what the flood had done, and chose to stay.
- Promoted to head coach in 1995. Started the Flood Memorial Game tradition in his first season — the home opener every year. Players wear black armbands. The 43 names are read before kickoff. The alma mater is sung instead of the fight song. He read the names himself for 22 years.
- Turned down Pittsburgh (DC) and Cincinnati (HC) offers. Said publicly only "Hollowridge needs a football team, and right now, that's me."
- Coached 1995-2017. 23 seasons. Won 6 conference titles. His teams were known for being physically and emotionally invested in a way other teams weren't.
- His wife Maria ran a community kitchen out of the football facility on home game days, feeding anyone in town who needed a meal. The kitchen still operates.
- Retired in 2017 at age 69 because he was tired. No dramatic departure.
- Died in 2023 of a stroke at age 75.
- Statue at the south end of the stadium. Depicts him on the sideline in his prime — clipboard, headset. After his death, the program added his name to the list honored at the Flood Memorial Game: "And we remember our coach."

**Current arc:** "Carry the town." Picking Hollowridge means inheriting a program that carries the grief of a wounded place. The team plays for the 43 names and for the coach who chose to belong.

### 4. Coral Bay Dolphins

**Colors:** Pink
**Region:** South Florida Atlantic coast, between West Palm Beach and Fort Lauderdale
**School:** Coral Bay University (private)
**Town:** Wealthy South Florida beach town. Modern, design-forward campus. ~12,000 students. ~28,000-seat stadium built ~2012, architecturally striking.
**Program identity:** Tech-and-money private university. Strong business and engineering programs, huge alumni network in tech and finance. Football program is well-funded but not the school's central identity. The program is built almost entirely on transfers — kids who got buried at SEC schools, 5-stars who flamed out at Power 5 programs, talented players looking for a second chance. "The island of misfit superstars."
**Talent tier:** Middle

**Ghost: Coach Jaylen "Jay" Tate (1981-)**
- Born in Atlanta, Georgia. Father was a high school football coach, mother was a sports lawyer.
- Played wide receiver at Vanderbilt 2000-2003. Moved into coaching immediately after.
- Climbed through coordinator jobs at SEC schools. Offensive coordinator at Houston (2014-2016) and Oregon (2017).
- Hired as Coral Bay's head coach in December 2017 at age 36. First Black head coach in school history.
- Saw the transfer portal coming before anyone else. Built half his roster from transfers by 2019. Built Coral Bay from 5-7 to 11-2 over 6 years, winning 2 conference titles.
- In early 2026, after Coral Bay agreed to join the Ember Eight, Tate took the head coaching job at Ole Miss. Timing was devastating — the school had just committed to a new conference and the architect left within weeks.
- Currently 44 years old and coaching Ole Miss. Winning. On TV every Saturday. Half the players he recruited are still on the Coral Bay roster, watching their old coach win at a bigger school every week.
- Has not returned to Coral Bay since leaving.

**Current arc:** "Rebuild from his model alone." The architect bailed for a bigger stage. His transfer-portal model is all that's left. The player inherits a program whose visionary left and has to figure out how to continue it without him — while watching him succeed elsewhere every Saturday.

### 5. Blackwater Serpents

**Colors:** Purple + black
**Region:** Atchafalaya Basin, Louisiana
**School:** Blackwater University
**Town:** Small college town on the edge of the Atchafalaya cypress swamp. ~3,000-5,000 people. French-Catholic heritage. Bilingual signs. Cajun French still spoken in some households. Game day = gumbo, boudin, zydeco, priest blessing. "Blackwater" = literal name of tannin-stained swamp water. Stadium ~30,000-35,000 seats, cypress swamp visible behind south endzone.
**Program identity:** Old, regional, French-Catholic in heritage. Founded late 1800s. Small to mid-sized (~8,000-12,000 students). Football is culturally important but not the dominant identity. The forgotten dynasty — briefly very good in the 1970s, then squeezed out when LSU started recruiting Cajun country.
**Talent tier:** Middle

**Ghost: Coach Ray Boudreaux (1935-2009)**
- Born in Lafayette, Louisiana. Cajun family, working class. Father was a welder.
- Played offensive line at Blackwater 1954-1957.
- Coached high school football in southern Louisiana through the 1960s.
- Head coach at Blackwater 1971-1990. 20 seasons. Built the program by recruiting south Louisiana high schools bigger programs ignored. Won 4 conference titles between 1975 and 1981. Made two New Year's Day bowl games (lost both).
- Decline started in the mid-80s when LSU began recruiting south Louisiana aggressively. Boudreaux's pipeline got squeezed.
- Retired in 1990 at age 55 after a 4-7 season. Said he was tired.
- Spent retirement in Lafayette. Did some regional radio color commentary. Showed up to about half the home games.
- Died in 2009 of a heart attack at 74. Normal Catholic funeral in Lafayette.
- Stadium concourse named after him (the Ray Boudreaux Concourse). No statue.

**Current arc:** "Get back to the 70s." The most grounded, least mythic ghost in the league. Boudreaux was a good coach who had a great run a long time ago. The current program is trying to recapture what he had. Picking Blackwater means signing up for a nostalgic revival — the town remembers when they were real, and they want to be real again.

### 6. Helix Salamanders

**Colors:** Deep navy + bright orange (NASA palette)
**Region:** Cypress Springs, Central Texas (Hill Country, Austin-San Antonio corridor)
**School:** Helix University (founded 1962)
**Town:** Fictional Hill Country town near the Edwards Aquifer. Limestone terrain, live oaks, springs. Campus is a mix of 1960s brutalist concrete and newer glass-and-limestone modern buildings.
**Program identity:** Science research university. Known for karst biology (cave ecosystems), water science, applied mathematics, computer science. The salamander mascot is a nerd-flex — the school's biology department literally studies Texas Blind Salamanders in the Edwards Aquifer. Football exists somewhat awkwardly within a science school. Players walk past Nobel Prize winners in the cafeteria.
**Talent tier:** Middle

**Ghost: Coach Marcus Chen (1958-)**
- Born in Berkeley, California. Father was a Stanford math professor.
- Played college football at Caltech in the late 70s. Graduated with a degree in applied mathematics.
- Spent the 1980s coaching high school football in Texas after his wife (a hydrogeologist) got a research position at Helix.
- Hired as Helix's head coach in 1992 at age 34. The hire was a joke — Helix was 2-9. Won immediately. 7-4 first year.
- Coached 1992-2007. 16 seasons. Won 11 conference titles. Made the national championship game once (lost).
- Famous for: writing a 1996 paper on fourth-down expected value that predates modern analytics by 15 years, never punting on 4th and short, running a defense based on game theory, and handing out opponent tendency printouts instead of pre-game pep talks.
- Retired in 2007 at age 49. Said he wanted to "do something else." Mike Leach-coded — his aim in life isn't 100% football.
- Currently teaches a probability theory class at Helix. Published academic papers since retiring. Has hobbies unrelated to football. Goes to maybe two games a year, sits in the regular stands.
- Will share football opinions if asked. They're usually counterintuitive and usually correct. The current coaching staff has his number. They don't call him.

**Current arc:** "Uncomfortable admiration." Chen is alive, down the hall, teaching math, and won't quite engage with the football program. The player has to decide whether to walk over and knock or build their own thing. Picking Helix means coaching in the shadow of a withdrawn genius who could help but won't volunteer.

### 7. Larkspur Pronghorns

**Colors:** Hunter green + cream
**Region:** Larkspur, Nebraska Panhandle (~50,000 people)
**School:** Larkspur State University (land-grant, founded 1860s-1870s)
**Town:** The state's flagship football program. The closest thing to a major league team in a 400-mile radius. Reinhardt Stadium capacity ~85,000 — holds more people than the entire town population on game days. Working farm on the edge of campus. The whole state drives in on Saturdays.
**Program identity:** The dynasty. The heavyweight. The team everyone else in the league organizes their hatred around. Unapologetically the favorite. Deep football tradition, massive stadium, generational fan loyalty.
**Talent tier:** Top

**Ghost: Coach Walter "Wally" Reinhardt (1958-)**
- Born in central Nebraska, German-American farming family. Father raised cattle and grew sugar beets.
- Played fullback at Larkspur State 1976-1979. Briefly tried out for an NFL team, didn't make it.
- Assistant coach 1980-1994, including 9 years as Larkspur's offensive coordinator.
- Head coach 1995-2024. 30 seasons. 5 national championships. The Ember Eight's most dominant pre-conference program.
- Famous for: power-spread offense, meticulous practice schedule unchanged in 30 years, recruiting almost exclusively from the Plains states, one-word press conference answers, hand-written personal letters to every recruit, collection of vintage farm tractors.
- Married once, to a woman from his hometown. Son is a high school principal, daughter is a veterinarian.
- Retired January 2026 at age 67 after winning his fifth national title. Cited "wanting to spend time with his grandchildren and his tractors." The retirement was a complete shock — no signals, no succession plan.
- Currently lives on a farm 20 minutes outside Larkspur. Has not given a single interview since retiring. Has been seen at zero games. Reportedly will attend the home opener of the inaugural Ember Eight season.

**Current arc:** "Preserve the dynasty in year one." Reinhardt just retired. The player is the first post-Reinhardt head coach. The fanbase expects you to win. The players were recruited by Reinhardt. Failure to win the inaugural Ember Eight title would be read as the end of the dynasty. Picking Larkspur means the highest-pressure job in the league.

### 8. Sacramento Raccoons

**Colors:** Charcoal, cream, lime green accent
**Region:** Downtown Sacramento, California
**School:** Sacramento Polytechnic ("Sac Poly," founded 2003)
**Town:** State capital of California. Downtown campus — no traditional quad, buildings interspersed with city blocks. ~25,000-seat modern stadium downtown. State capitol building visible from the upper deck.
**Program identity:** Genuinely new. Founded 2003 as a California higher-ed experiment — polytechnic/design/engineering focus, ~18,000 students. Design-school faculty. Progressive, queer-friendly, nerdy student body. Football founded 2014 after student lobbying. FCS no-scholarship start, moved to FBS in 2020. Only 6 years of FBS football at the inaugural Ember Eight season — youngest program by a wide margin. Raccoons mascot = student-voted 2014 joke that became beloved. Real mascot raccoon named "Bandit" on campus.
**Talent tier:** Bottom

**Ghost: None.**
The program is too new. Three previous head coaches in 12 years, all left for various reasons. The player is the 4th head coach. There's nobody on the wall. Nobody to live up to.

**Current arc:** "Build something from nothing. Become the first ghost." The only team in the league with no history to carry. The torch hasn't been passed yet — the program is waiting for someone to light it. Picking Sac Poly is a challenge run: worst facilities, no recruiting budget, no tradition, fearless analytical playstyle. The player who picks Sac Poly is signing up to write the founding chapter of a program that doesn't exist yet.

## Research Audit Notes (April 11, 2026)

All team lore was audited against real-world geography, culture, and history. Corrections applied:

- **Vermont:** St. Marlowe sized to ~6,000-8,000 people (consistent with real NEK town scale; largest real NEK town is St. Johnsbury at 7,600).
- **Ridgemont:** Feral hogs confirmed as massive real problem in Arkansas (~200,000+ by 2015, present in nearly every county). Tyson + mill town economy is authentic Ozark pattern.
- **Hollowridge:** Flood moved from 1997 to November 1989 (no real nationally-known WV flood that year; 1997 had no real parallel). Vecchio's timeline adjusted — he arrived as DC in 1992, three years after the flood, making him an outsider who chose a wounded program rather than someone who survived the trauma with them.
- **Blackwater:** Atchafalaya Basin confirmed as largest US river swamp. French-Catholic Cajun culture, blackwater ecology, all confirmed accurate. Boudreaux's Lafayette background is authentic.
- **Helix:** Moved from Houston to Central Texas Hill Country (Austin-San Antonio corridor). Texas Blind Salamanders live in the Edwards Aquifer near San Marcos, 150+ miles from Houston — the Houston location was geographically incoherent. Chen's wife changed from NASA engineer to hydrogeologist.
- **Larkspur:** Moved to Nebraska Panhandle (real pronghorn territory is western NE). Town sized to ~50,000 (bigger than any real Panhandle town but plausible as a fictional flagship city). Larkspur confirmed as a real native wildflower that pronghorns actually eat.
- **Coral Bay:** "Silicon Beach" language softened to "tech-and-money private" (Silicon Beach is more an LA concept than Florida).
- **Sacramento:** No corrections needed.
