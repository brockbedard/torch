# Market Research: Football Deckbuilders

This report explores existing football-themed deckbuilding games across Steam, iOS, and Android, and specifically examines tactical mechanics like "Audibles," "Personnel Groupings," and "Momentum Multipliers."

## 1. Existing Football Deckbuilders by Platform

### Steam (PC)
*   **NUTMEG!**: A retro-styled soccer management game using card packs to enhance squads. Matches involve tactical card-based control.
*   **Bobo Blitz**: A roguelike American Football deckbuilder focused on energy management and field movement.
*   **Squiggle Football**: Inspired by Australian Rules Football (AFL). A management sim with roguelike elements for building teams and tactics.
*   **Wild Rumble**: An arcade soccer game with roguelike skill upgrades and team development.
*   **Football Drama**: A narrative-heavy soccer manager where life choices and tactical cards determine match outcomes.

### iOS & Android
*   **Football Drama**: (Same as above) Cross-platform narrative and tactical card game.
*   **Sunday League Deckbuilder (Android)**: A community project centered on building a deck of football actions to rise through the ranks of Sunday League.
*   **Football Referee Simulator**: While primarily a sim, it uses card-like decision mechanics in its management and federation modes.

## 2. Specific Mechanics Analysis: "Technician Football"

The specific tactical mechanics requested are cornerstone features of **Technician Football** (by Technician Games), a tactical American football card game often played on Tabletop Simulator.

### Personnel Groupings
*   **Implementation**: This is the foundation of every play. Players choose cards representing specific personnel sets (e.g., "11 Personnel," "12 Personnel," "21 Personnel").
*   **Effect**: The chosen grouping limits which "Play Cards" (Pass, Run, Screen) are eligible for use and provides stat bonuses or penalties based on the defensive grouping matchup. It forces the player to consider the "math" of the field before selecting a play.

### Audibles
*   **Implementation**: Used after initial personnel or play commitment phases. 
*   **Effect**: Allows a player to swap their current play for a different one in their hand. This simulates "reading the defense" at the line of scrimmage, providing a reactive layer to the pre-snap strategy.

### Momentum Multipliers
*   **Implementation**: Tracks the game's flow via a momentum meter or token system.
*   **Effect**: Successful actions (big gains, sacks, turnovers) generate Momentum. This momentum is then applied as a **multiplier** to future play results. For example, high momentum might increase a "Run" yardage card by 1.5x or grant a defensive "Stop" a higher probability of success. It mimics the psychological and physical "on a roll" feeling in real football.

## 3. Notable Competitors in the Genre
*   **Blood Bowl: Team Manager (Tabletop)**: Though currently out of print, it is the most influential "Sports Deckbuilder." It uses a recruitment system similar to *Legendary: A Marvel Deck Building Game*, where players build their team over several matches to win tournaments.
*   **Counter Attack**: A highly tactical, turn-based board/digital game focusing on positional strategy and card-driven movement.

## 4. Key Takeaways for Market Gaps
*   **American Football is Underserved**: Most digital "Football" deckbuilders focus on Soccer (Association Football). *Bobo Blitz* is one of the few recent entries for American Football.
*   **Sim vs. Arcade**: There is a gap for high-fidelity tactical simulations like *Technician Football* in a standalone digital format (not just Tabletop Simulator), specifically utilizing complex personnel and momentum systems.
