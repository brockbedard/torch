# Football Snap Narrative and TORCH Reveal Performance

This document outlines the cinematic beats of a football play and recommends a 2.5s "Reveal Script" for TORCH to connect performance results to the narrative flow of a snap.

## The Cinematic Beats of a Football Snap (4-5s)

A real football play is a microcosm of storytelling, typically lasting between 4 to 5 seconds. To make the "Reveal Performance" feel impactful, it must mirror these beats:

### 1. The Read (0.0s - 1.0s)
*   **The Narrative:** Tension before the snap followed by the immediate reaction. The QB identifies the coverage; the linebackers step up to fill gaps.
*   **Cinematic Feel:** Anticipation and focus.
*   **Visuals:** Low-angle "trench" shots or tight focus on the QB's eyes/ball.

### 2. The Conflict (1.0s - 3.0s)
*   **The Narrative:** The collision of forces. Linemen engage in a power struggle; receivers battle for position; the QB navigates a collapsing pocket.
*   **Cinematic Feel:** Chaos, kinetic energy, and high stakes.
*   **Visuals:** Motion blur, camera shakes on hits, and fast-cutting between key matchups.

### 3. The Result (3.0s - 5.0s)
*   **The Narrative:** The climax and resolution. The ball is either caught, dropped, or the QB is sacked.
*   **Cinematic Feel:** Release of tension, triumph, or sudden impact.
*   **Visuals:** Wide shots to show the field gain or extreme close-ups on the catch/hit.

---

## TORCH 2.5s "Reveal Script" Recommendation

For a compressed 2.5-second reveal that maintains the narrative punch of a full play, TORCH should utilize specific audio/visual cues to tell the story of the performance result.

### 0.0s - 0.5s: The Snap (The Read)
*   **Audio:** Sharp, rhythmic "Hut-Hut!" followed by the mechanical "click" of the ball leaving the center's hands.
*   **Visual:** Rapid zoom from a wide "All-22" view into a focused "Broadcast" angle. The screen borders pulse slightly with the team's primary color.

### 0.5s - 1.5s: The Development (The Conflict)
*   **Audio:** Ambient "white noise" of pads clashing and turf crunching. A rising synthetic "shepard tone" to build tension.
*   **Visual:** Dynamic "Speed Lines" or peripheral blur to simulate the momentum of the play.

### 1.5s - 2.5s: The Reveal (The Result)
The ending varies based on the performance outcome:

#### Outcome A: The Stuff (Defensive Success)
*   **Narrative:** A run play stopped for no gain or a sack.
*   **Audio:** A heavy, resonant **"THUD"** with a sub-bass drop. All ambient noise cuts out for a split second.
*   **Visual:** A violent screen shake (camera impact) and a "color drain" effect that emphasizes the hit.

#### Outcome B: The Big Play (Offensive Success - Deep Pass)
*   **Narrative:** A long completion or a touchdown pass.
*   **Audio:** A high-pitched, metallic **"ZIP"** or "whistle" sound as the ball travels, followed by an explosive crowd roar and stadium horn.
*   **Visual:** The camera "tracks" an invisible projectile across the screen with a light trail effect. Bright, high-contrast flash on the "catch."

#### Outcome C: The Precision Gain (Efficiency)
*   **Narrative:** A short, reliable 5-yard completion.
*   **Audio:** Two quick, clean **"TAPS"** (catch and foot-plant) followed by a satisfying "ding" of a first-down marker.
*   **Visual:** A clean, green "Success" overlay with a smooth camera pan following the yard line gain.

## Implementation Notes
- **Haptics:** Sync mobile haptics to the "Result" beat (Short buzz for "The Precision Gain," heavy jolt for "The Stuff," long vibration for "The Big Play").
- **Dynamic Timing:** The 2.5s window is the "Sweet Spot" for user retention in gacha/performance reveals, ensuring the player feels the "hit" without the experience dragging.
