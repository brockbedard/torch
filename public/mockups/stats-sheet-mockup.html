NEW DESIGN NOTES

This is the redesigned stats sheet that opens when you tap the scorebug. Expanded from the current basic table to a full broadcast-style game stats screen.

WHAT'S NEW

  • Big score cards at top with TD/FG/TO breakdown per team
  • Key Metrics hero row — 4 tiles with mini tug-of-war bars (EPA, Turnover Margin, 3rd Down %, Explosive Plays)
  • Passing / Rushing / Defense sections with inline bar comparisons on every stat — instantly see who's winning each category
  • Per-stat "win" highlight — the leading team's number gets colored in their accent + subtle glow
  • Compact drives list with EPA per drive + colored result pills (TD/FG/TO/PUNT)
  • TORCH economy section for points + cards used

STAT CATEGORIES

  • Score cards (hero) — score + TD/FG/TO counts per team
  • Key Metrics — EPA, TO margin, 3rd down %, explosives
  • Passing — comp/att, yds, TDs, INTs
  • Rushing — att, yds, YPC, TDs
  • Defense — sacks, TFLs, PBUs, forced TOs
  • Drives — compact scrollable list w/ EPA + result pills
  • Economy — TORCH points, cards used

WHERE SHOULD IT LIVE?

Current state has two stats destinations that overlap: the Clipboard (swipe-up bottom sheet, 4 stats) and this StatsSheet (scorebug tap, detailed table). Confusing.

My recommendation: kill the Clipboard bottom sheet, make this the single canonical stats destination. The new design starts with the same 4 hero metrics (EPA, TO margin, 3rd down, explosives) then scrolls into the detailed box score. Everything the Clipboard showed is still there, plus the detailed tables.

Scorebug tap is a more discoverable trigger than a thin drag handle. Users expect to tap the score to see stats — that's a standard sports broadcast affordance.

ALTERNATIVE: KEEP BOTH

If you want to keep the Clipboard for quick-glance during live play and reserve this for full post-drive review, that works too. The Clipboard becomes "at-a-glance" and this becomes "full box score." Both reachable.
